Background

The topic

This topic aim to let people always adopt the right cats – never buy them from a breeder or pet shop to solve Australia’ Feral Cat Problem.

The problem

Cats are good family companions, but when they live feral or roam outdoors unattended, they face many dangers, including infectious diseases, speeding cars, poisons, dogs, and cruel humans. It is estimated that feral cats kill 75 million native animals every night across Australia, including birds, frogs, small mammals, and reptiles. (Matters & night, 2020)

One study in Victoria revealed that 13 per cent of cats had a litter prior to being desexed. ("Early-age desexing of dogs and cats", 2020) Unfortunately, lots of kittens do not find loving homes. Many are surrendered to shelters, which are already overrun with animals, and are euthanized. Worse still, countless other cats are abandoned to fend for themselves, sometimes joining feral cat colonies. (Feral cats, 2020)

The need

Choosing to adopt a cat or kitten is an exciting time. However, there are many things to consider before adopting a pet to help ensure you can properly provide the right care and attention to your cat or kitten. This requires a guide. Matching the right people to the right cat is important, that is why to set an adoption process. Take your time to think about what type of cat you are after, and what type of cat is right for you and your family. ("Choosing the Right Cat Personality for Your Lifestyle | Hill's Pet", 2016)

Therefore, this tutorial addresses the need of people who want to have a cat but do not know which kind of cat they want.

The user

The target users of this website are those who want to raise cats but do not know what type of cats to raise. In particular, people’s demand for domestic pets is increasing. Cats are also very good friends of humans. However, for such an adoption website, it is important to ensure that the user experience is clear and simple. These aspects should attract all potential users, especially those who want to raise cats and are caring.

Questionnaire

Q&A

In order to fully understand the needs of potential users, I made a questionnaire in which I asked four groups of people, namely, elderly people living in their own lives, international students, people with families, and adults working in their own lives. Twenty-three people filled out my survey questionnaire, provided that they all wanted to raise a cat.

Persona profile

I selected four people from 23 people who filled out the survey questionnaire for the interview. This is their persona.

Sketches

Moving onto the layout design, the first iteration was that of sketches for the landing page. A strong landing page is important to hold on to users, set a theme and establish a clear goal and route toward.

Choosing the cat that suits you is a very important thing. So I dedicated a page to introduce the types of feral cats I own. Photos are a good way to convey the image of a cat. So I took photos of each cat.

The final confirmation address interface is also very important. It includes the cat’s information, the address to be delivered, and our contact information. So I also did a few iterations.

Designs

Contain wireframes detailing the overall form and structure of your proposed website and mockups showing the visual and aesthetic design of proposed website.

Wireframes

I chose two layout methods for my first landing page, one is to put the image on the top, the other is to put the image on the left of the brand. But considering the layout of mobile phones, I currently think it would be more reasonable to put pictures on top of the text.

After going through the login page, I created the home page. It introduces the information of wild cats and it is also a problem of picture placement. Because the content is basically easy to understand, there is no confusing place to understand the information. So I designed two iterations to plan my wireframe.

After passing the homepage, you need to sign the insurance first. Because insurance is very important, every wildcat has its own insurance when it is adopted to ensure their health. The interface should also be concise and easy to understand, so that users can see information intuitively.

Choosing a cat that suits you is also very important, so choosing a cat's wireframe should be clear and intuitive. People can directly see the looks and breeds of cats, as well as the breeder's understanding of their personalities. I chose two methods, the feel of a horizontal poster, and the scrolling like a post-it note.

The final confirmation page is also clear and warm. And left our contact information. The location of the contact information is eye-catching, so people can find it instantly. I also consider the issue of typesetting conversion between computer users and mobile phones and users.
